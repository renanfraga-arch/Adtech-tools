Key Insights
BEESCEXP — Leads at 90.9 avg, front-loaded delivery

Highest average on the FlowOps basis. Sprints 185–187 alone accounted for 660 CP (282 + 221 + 157) — S185 is the single highest sprint value across all teams. Zero activated in sprints 189–192, indicating a significant pipeline build-up heading into the current period.

BEESADT — Most consistent, 10.8 CP behind BEESCEXP

Only team with CP activated in all 10 sprints. The 80.1 avg sits 10.8 CP behind BEESCEXP — a wider gap than the 5.8 the sprint-field method showed. Sprint 184 was the peak (162 CP), driven by issues planned in S183 that activated within the S184 window.

BEESCATB — 3rd at 56.5 avg, 23.6 below BEESADT

Sprint 183 is the actual peak (125 CP); Sprint 189 (119 CP) was close behind. Sprint 184 shows zero — no CP-bearing issues reached a terminal status in that 2-week window. The FlowOps avg of 56.5 is lower than the 61.5 the sprint-field method showed.

BEESCK — Different scale entirely (11.4 avg)

Smallest footprint across all 10 sprints. Sprint 184 was the standout (48 CP). Three sprints show zero activated. CP is applied selectively — making this team's average roughly 7× below BEESADT and not directly comparable to teams where CP is assigned broadly.
